Books My Parents Made Me Read

100 Year Of Solitude

One Hundred Years of Solitude is a 1967 novel by Colombian author Gabriel García Márquez that tells the multi-generational story of the Buendía family.

Baudolino

Baudolino is a 2000 novel by Umberto Eco about the adventures of a man named Baudolino in the known and mythical Christian world of the 12th century.

Brothers Karamazov

Set in 19th-century Russia, The Brothers Karamazov is a passionate philosophical novel that discusses questions of God, free will, and morality.

For Whom The Bells Toll

For Whom the Bell Tolls is a novel by Ernest Hemingway published in 1940. It tells the story of Robert Jordan. A dynamiter, he is assigned to blow up a bridge during an attack on the city of Segovia.

All Quiet On The Western Front

All Quiet on the Western Front is a novel by Erich Maria Remarque, a German veteran of World War I. The book describes the German soldiers' physical and mental trauma during the war.

Moby-Dick

Moby-Dick; or, The Whale is an 1851 novel by American writer Herman Melville. The book is the maniacal quest of vengeance led by capitan Ahab against Moby Dick.

All Tomorrows

All Tomorrows is a 2006 work of science fiction and speculative evolution written and illustrated by Nemo Ramjet. It explores a hypothetical future path of human evolution set from the near future to a billion years from the present.

A Clockwork Orange

A Clockwork Orange is a dystopian novel by English writer Anthony Burgess, published in 1962. It is set in a near-future society that has a youth subculture of extreme violence. Alex, narrates his violence and his experiences with authorities reforming him.
